# Summary Report – Session 2: "Breaking Through Cloud Barriers & Pushing Limits with Hackathons"

### Event Objectives

- **Eliminate financial fear and technical barriers** for beginners learning cloud computing (AWS) through gamified learning methods and simulation tools.
- **Inspire and clarify the core value** of Hackathons as a vehicle for hands-on, real-world project experience.
- **Provide important perspectives** on system operations workflows, psychology, and diversifying the worldview of technology practitioners.

### Key Highlights

#### 1. Level Up Your AWS Skills with Cloud Quest and Floci

*Speaker: Huynh Thai Linh*

Beginners learning AWS often feel anxious and constantly check their bills, fearing they may forget to delete resources after practice sessions, resulting in unexpected charges. To address this, the speaker introduced 2 tools:

- **AWS Cloud Quest:** An RPG (role-playing game) developed by AWS itself. The game combines theory and hands-on practice, helping learners build and validate Cloud skills by solving real-world scenarios for virtual characters in the game.
- **Floci AWS Testing:** A completely free, open-source Local Cloud Emulator. It replaces bulky, paid simulation tools with an extremely fast startup time and minimal resource consumption. However, Floci's limitations include incomplete AWS service coverage and some services that only return mock data (fake results) — it cannot fully replace a real Cloud environment.

---

#### 2. Hackathon

*Speakers: Huynh An Khuong, Mai Quoc Anh, Nguyen Tran Nguyen Quan*

A Hackathon is a team-based software development competition held within a limited timeframe (typically 24–48 hours). It is a "programming marathon" where team members eat, sleep, and code together at the venue to transform a raw idea into a working prototype or MVP. Participating in a Hackathon is an excellent opportunity to step out of one's comfort zone and directly engage with real-world projects under pressure.

---

#### 3. Why We Always Need Confidence

*Speaker: Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu*

This talk celebrated inner strength and the importance of building a confident mindset for people working in technology. Confidence does not come from being perfect — it comes from daring to start, daring to fail, and persisting through learning. This is the foundational quality that prevents tech practitioners from being held back by the fear of failure when confronting new challenges.

---

#### 4. Vietnamese Astrology (Tử Vi Đại Việt)

*Speaker: Nghia Tran*

An introduction to the **tuvidaiviet.com** website project, independently developed by the speaker's team. It is a platform that digitizes traditional Vietnamese numerology and astrology knowledge, providing online tools for generating natal charts, looking up and interpreting destiny readings. This is an inspiring example of applying web technology to solve niche problems, modernize cultural heritage, and open up creative startup directions.

---

#### 5. The Hidden Iceberg of a Project: DevOps Before Disaster

*Speaker: Tran Minh Quan*

This talk emphasized the critical role of DevOps in keeping systems stable over the long term. Without solid operational processes and infrastructure control, a system harbors countless hidden risks (icebergs) waiting to surface. DevOps is a protective layer that must be built before disaster strikes — not after.

---

#### 6. The Iceberg of Procrastination

*Speaker: Khac Uy Pham*

An analysis of the psychology behind procrastination — a phenomenon that appears simple on the surface but is in reality a hidden "iceberg" driven by deep underlying causes. The talk helped attendees identify specific behavioral patterns and provided mental frameworks for managing time more effectively, rather than waiting until the last minute.

---

### Key Takeaways

Session 2 helped resolve the biggest blind spot when learning Cloud: the fear of spending money. Discovering Cloud Quest and the Floci simulation tool provides an extremely safe and practical learning path. Furthermore, the concepts introduced around Hackathons were highly motivating — reinforcing that continuously coding at high intensity and completing a Prototype within a short timeframe is one of the best ways to sharpen real-world skills.

---

### Applying to Work

For the ongoing online shoe-store website project, instead of worrying about costs when deploying the system to the cloud, using the Floci emulator in a local environment is the perfect stepping stone. It allows deploying and testing Node.js/Express APIs for product catalog management or image upload logic, then validating them with continuous Postman requests — all without incurring any charges.

Additionally, the "programming marathon" spirit of Hackathons is an ideal framework to apply when building the chess web game (using Spring Boot). Setting a self-imposed 24–48 hour time block over the weekend will create positive pressure, helping maintain sharp focus to complete the chessboard logic and produce a stable MVP.

---

### Event Experience

The atmosphere of the second session was vibrant and captivating. The mix of hard technical solutions like Cloud emulation, the passionate intensity of Hackathons, and the fresh perspective from the Tử Vi Đại Việt project created a remarkably well-rounded workshop. Witnessing the tangible products built by the speakers was incredibly inspiring, motivating me to enhance my personal projects and embrace new challenges.
